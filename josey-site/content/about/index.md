About Me

<!--more-->

Hi, I'm Josey Barlow — a senior Electrical Engineering student, and this is my little corner of the internet.

By day, I'm deep in circuits, signal processing, and whatever my latest project throws at me. By night (and most weekends), you'll usually find me with a crochet hook in hand or something rising in the oven. This site is where all of that lives in one place: the engineering work I'm proud of, and the slower, hands-on hobbies that keep me sane while I'm getting there.

## What you'll find here

- **Engineering** — projects, coursework highlights, and things I've learned along the way as I finish my EE degree
- **Baking** — recipes, experiments, and the occasional kitchen disaster
- **Crochet** — patterns, works in progress, and yarn rabbit holes

I started this site mostly so I'd stop losing track of my own notes and photos. If something here is useful to you too, even better.

## Get in touch

Feel free to reach out if you want to talk shop — electrical engineering, baking science (yes, it's basically applied chemistry), or crochet patterns. You can find my contact details and socials linked around the site.

Thanks for stopping by!
